# Six-Month Week-by-Week Quantitative Developer (Quant Dev) Roadmap: Excel-Ready To-Do List

---

## Introduction

Becoming a **quantitative developer (quant dev)** is a rigorous, multidisciplinary journey that blends advanced programming, mathematical modeling, financial theory, and systems engineering. The role is pivotal in algorithmic trading, risk management, and financial analytics, requiring mastery of both software development and quantitative finance. This six-month, week-by-week plan is designed for individuals with a computer science background and basic programming skills, aiming to transition into a quant dev role in top-tier trading firms, hedge funds, or financial technology companies—especially in the Indian market (Mumbai focus).

The roadmap is structured for **full-time learners** and is suitable for easy export into Excel, with columns for Week, Focus Area, Tasks, Resources, and Notes. Each week builds on the previous, ensuring a logical progression from foundational skills to advanced topics, practical projects, and interview preparation. The plan integrates recommendations from leading quant practitioners, academic programs, and industry job requirements.

## Detailed Section Analysis and Guidance

### Foundations and Environment Setup

The first week is crucial for **goal setting** and establishing a robust coding environment. Quant devs must identify target companies (e.g., Mumbai-based firms like Quadeye, AlphaGrep, Tower Research) and understand the skills required for their roles. Setting up Python (Anaconda, VSCode, Jupyter) and reviewing core syntax ensures a smooth start. Joining quant communities (Discord, LinkedIn, GitHub) provides networking opportunities and access to peer support.

### Data Structures, Algorithms, and Coding Practice

Weeks 2–4 focus on **data structures and algorithms (DSA)**, which are essential for both quant interviews and building efficient trading systems. Mastery of arrays, linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, graphs, heaps, and hash tables is expected. Implementing these in Python and practicing on platforms like LeetCode and GeeksforGeeks builds problem-solving skills and interview readiness. Emphasis on time and space complexity analysis prepares candidates for technical assessments.

### C++ for Quantitative Finance

C++ remains the backbone of high-performance trading systems due to its speed and control over memory. Weeks 5–6 cover **C++ fundamentals, modern features (C++11/14/17), STL, templates, concurrency, and performance optimization**. Building small projects and profiling code helps learners appreciate the trade-offs between Python and C++ in quant applications.

### Numerical Methods, Linear Algebra, and Scientific Computing

Numerical methods (NumPy, SciPy, Eigen) and **linear algebra** are foundational for modeling portfolios, risk, and factor exposures. Learners should implement matrix operations, eigenvalue decompositions, SVD, and PCA, applying these to real financial datasets. Understanding the mathematical backbone of finance enables effective model building and risk analysis.

### Probability, Statistics, and Econometrics

Weeks 9–10 delve into **probability theory, distributions, statistics, and econometrics**. These topics underpin risk modeling, option pricing, and trading strategy evaluation. Learners should practice probability puzzles, hypothesis testing, regression analysis, and time series basics, using Python for simulations and statistical tests.

### Stochastic Calculus, Derivatives, and Financial Mathematics

Stochastic calculus (Brownian motion, Ito's lemma, SDEs) and **financial mathematics (derivatives, Black-Scholes, Greeks)** are central to quant dev roles in derivatives pricing and risk management. Coding option pricing models and calculating Greeks in Python/C++ demonstrates practical understanding and prepares candidates for technical interviews.

### Fixed Income, Monte Carlo, and Volatility Modeling

Fixed income modeling (bonds, yield curves, duration, convexity) and **Monte Carlo methods** are vital for risk and pricing in both equity and fixed income markets. Learners should implement simulations, variance reduction techniques, and volatility forecasting (GARCH) using real or simulated market data.

### Machine Learning and Deep Learning for Finance

Weeks 16–17 introduce **machine learning (ML), deep learning, RNNs, LSTMs, and Transformers** for financial prediction and time series analysis. Learners should focus on supervised learning, feature engineering, cross-validation, and advanced models for time series forecasting. Documenting model selection and performance is essential for interviews and portfolio presentation.

### Reinforcement Learning and Algorithmic Trading

Reinforcement learning (RL) is increasingly used in **algorithmic trading**. Implementing RL agents (Q-learning, policy gradients) and backtesting trading strategies in Python (OpenAI Gym, gym-anytrading) provides hands-on experience with cutting-edge quant techniques.

### Market Microstructure and High-Frequency Trading

Understanding **market microstructure (order books, price-time priority, latency, slippage)** is critical for building execution algorithms and trading systems. Learners should build order book simulators and analyze market impact, using both simulated and real tick data.

### Systems Design, Data Engineering, and Quant Platform Architecture

Weeks 20–21 focus on **systems design, architecture, databases, and data engineering** for quant platforms. Learners should study scalable system design, CEP, OMS, risk modules, and implement ETL pipelines for high-frequency data. Understanding time-series databases (kdb+, InfluxDB) and optimizing for latency and throughput is essential.

### Software Engineering, Testing, and Performance Profiling

Software engineering best practices (testing, CI/CD, code reviews) and **performance profiling** (gprof, cProfile, py-spy) ensure reliability and speed in quant systems. Learners should automate tests, builds, and profile code for bottlenecks, focusing on real-world trading constraints.

### Capstone Projects and Portfolio Development

Weeks 24–26 are dedicated to **practical projects**: end-to-end quant strategy with backtest, order book simulator with execution algorithms, and quant platform components (ETL, dashboard, tests). Documenting code, results, and lessons learned prepares candidates for interviews and portfolio presentation.

### Interview Preparation, Resume, and Job Market Research

Interview preparation (mental math, probability puzzles, mock interviews), resume building, and **job market research (Mumbai/India quant firms)** are critical for landing quant dev roles. Tailoring applications, leveraging GitHub portfolios, and networking with recruiters increases chances of success.

### Weekly Schedule Templates and Study Tips

Maintaining a **weekly study schedule**, tracking progress, and adapting based on strengths and weaknesses ensures consistency and balance. Productivity and learning strategies should be reviewed regularly for optimal results.
